The Current System

The current system is only a concept. There is no actual system in existence.

The Proposed System: Needs

The new system needs to be able to accomplish the following:

-          Allow different users to have different profiles within the game

-          Give different learning content to each user depending on the level they are at

-          Give users the ability to change the level of difficulty of their learning content

-          Display the progress of the user

-          Allow the user to play games during sections where learning content is not offered

-          Teach the user about various topics including mathematics, language arts, science, and history

The Proposed System: Users and Modes of Operation

Users

The only user class is the student. Students will login at the main screen to resume their progress or to start a new course plan. Students will then be taught the topics listed above, after which they will be tested on those topics.  Students will be able to move on to the next topic or review (or be forced to review, depending on their test score) the previous topic. After a section of content is progressed through, students will be able to play games to take a break.

Modes of Operation

The first mode of operation is login mode. Users will be presented with a login screen, giving them the option to either login and resume their progress or to register for a new account.

The second mode of operation is the course selection mode. New users will be immediately dropped into lesson mode and will therefore bypass this mode. Existing users will first be presented with a status screen, allowing them to either continue a paused lesson, move on to another lesson, or review a previous lesson.

The third mode of operation is lesson mode. Users will be introduced to learning content in a virtual school setting. Two lessons will occur before the lunch break and two lessons after. Lessons will be presented in a two part format. Part one will introduce or expand on a topic and part two will test on what was taught. At the end of a section or day, users will be given a report card detailing their progress on each topic. Users who do not score satisfactorily on a given topic will have to attend remedial lessons after class to ensure they know the material well enough to move on. All progress is automatically saved. After a week of such sections, users will progress onto weekend mode or exam mode, depending on which week they have completed and if they pass the review test at the end of the week.

The fourth mode of operation is weekend mode. Users will not be presented with straight material during this time, but be allowed to play games, which may or may not include a learning component.

The fifth mode of operation is exam mode. These will occur at the end of every semester of sections and satisfactory completion of the tests given will be required to progress back to lesson mode or to graduation mode, depending on which semester was completed. Users who do not pass the tests will be prompted to review the past material and be given another opportunity to take the test.

The sixth mode of operation is graduation mode. Once both exam modes of a given class year are completed, the user will progress to graduation mode, where he/she will be congratulated on their progress, given a diploma, and given the opportunity to either progress further if such content exists, or given the end game

The Proposed System: Operational Scenarios

The normal operational scenario would consist of users logging in or registering, going through lessons as planned, scoring satisfactorily on or redoing tests, and eventually completing the content.

One atypical scenario would be if the logon system was not secure enough, allowing users to access an account that did not belong to them.

Another would be if a bug prevented a user from progressing to the next section, thereby trapping them in an infinite loop of repeated content. 

One more such scenario would be if the user was unable to, due to a bug in their profile, move back to content they had previously completed, or move on to the section they were currently on

The Proposed System: Operational Features

Must Have:

-          A profile system

-          A complete set of learning content for all targeted age groups

-          A system to allow users to move between content

-          Progress assessment

-          An endgame

-          Basic animation and graphics

-          Basic soundtrack

Would Like to Have:

-          Games (1 to 4)

-          An online component for scores (for classes or games)

-          Full animation in certain sections

-          Fuller soundtrack

 

The Proposed System: Expected Impacts

We expect that students will be able to use our application as a supplement to their existing education, to build on concepts and expand their knowledge. We would like for teachers to implement the program into their existing courses (perhaps as homework) and expect that they would be able to do so easily due to the free availability of the program on the internet.

 

The Proposed System: Analysis

Expected Improvements:

-          No previous system exists. Thus, anything added will be an improvement.

Disadvantages:

-          Learning content may not be expansive enough to fully engage and educate students

Limitations:

-          The content required to furnish the game is not the most readily available.

Risks:

-          Games may not engage students enough to encourage them to continue playing.

Alternatives and Tradeoffs:

-          Tone down the scope of the program to not include so many age levels.

-          Change the focus of the game from a primary teaching aid to a game that tricks students into learning.
